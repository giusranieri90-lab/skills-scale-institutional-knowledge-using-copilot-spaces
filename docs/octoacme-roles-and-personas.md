# OctoAcme Personas

This document defines typical roles and responsibilities used in OctoAcme project docs and exercises.

---

## Developers

### Role Summary
Developers design, build, test, and deliver software components. They collaborate with product and project leads to implement features that meet acceptance criteria and quality standards.

### Responsibilities
- Implement features and fixes to meet acceptance criteria
- Write and maintain tests and documentation
- Participate in design and code reviews
- Assist in estimating and planning work
- Help identify technical risks and propose mitigations

### Goals
- Deliver reliable, maintainable code
- Reduce cycle time from idea to production
- Maintain high test coverage and observability

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint planning
- PR descriptions and code review comments
- Technical design docs when needed

---

## Product Managers

### Role Summary
Product Managers define what should be built to deliver customer and business value. They own the product vision, prioritize the backlog, and measure outcomes.

### Responsibilities
- Define problem statements and success metrics
- Prioritize the roadmap and backlog
- Collaborate with stakeholders and engineering on trade-offs
- Validate solutions through user research and metrics

### Goals
- Maximize customer value and impact
- Make clear, data-driven prioritization decisions
- Ensure product-market fit and usability

### Typical Communication
- Weekly alignment with PM and engineering leads
- Roadmap updates and stakeholder briefings
- Acceptance criteria and feature specs

---

## Project Managers

### Role Summary
Project Managers coordinate delivery activities, manage schedules, risks, and communications. They enable the team to deliver on commitments efficiently.

### Responsibilities
- Create and maintain project plans and timelines
- Manage risks, dependencies, and resource constraints
- Facilitate meetings (kickoff, planning, retrospectives)
- Ensure consistent project documentation and status reporting
- Coordinate cross-team and stakeholder communication

### Goals
- Deliver projects on time and within scope
- Minimize unplanned work and escalations
- Maintain transparency and alignment across stakeholders

### Typical Communication
- Weekly status updates and stakeholder reports
- Risk registers and decision logs
- Coordination via project boards and meeting facilitation

## Program Integrator

### Role Summary
The Program Integrator coordinates across multiple projects and teams to maintain program-level alignment, manage cross-project dependencies, and surface risks and schedule conflicts to the PMO.

### Responsibilities
- Maintain the program-level roadmap and dependency map.
- Coordinate sequencing across projects and resolve schedule conflicts.
- Escalate cross-project risks to the Program Manager / PMO.
- Monitor milestones that span multiple projects and communicate impacts.

### Goals
- Reduce cross-project delays and rework.
- Ensure dependent work is synchronized and visible.
- Improve program-level predictability.

### Typical Communication
- Weekly cross-project syncs, dependency dashboards, and status briefings to PMO and stakeholders.

### Interactions / RACI examples
- Responsible: Program Integrator (coordinate cross-project timeline)
- Accountable: Program Manager / PMO
- Consulted: Engineering Liaison, Security/Compliance Owner
- Informed: Stakeholder Representative

## Delivery Coordinator

### Role Summary
The Delivery Coordinator organizes and oversees release activities, coordinates deployment windows, and ensures runbooks and approvals (QA, security) are in place.

### Responsibilities
- Maintain release schedules and coordinate deployment windows across teams.
- Ensure release checklists and runbooks are complete before deployment.
- Coordinate required sign-offs (QA, security, product).
- Communicate release impacts and rollback plans.

### Goals
- Reduce deployment incidents and hotfixes.
- Ensure repeatable, low-risk releases.

### Typical Communication
- Release readiness meetings, deployment retrospectives, and broadcast release notes.

---

## Engineering Liaison

### Role Summary
The Engineering Liaison represents engineering concerns in planning and decision-making, clarifies technical constraints, and helps translate technical trade-offs to PMs and stakeholders.

### Responsibilities
- Provide technical context during planning and prioritization.
- Surface technical debt and architectural risks for prioritization.
- Help scope technically feasible deliverables with Project/Product Managers.

### Goals
- Improve planning accuracy and technical-risk visibility.
- Balance delivery speed with architectural health.

### Typical Communication
- Planning sessions, technical impact notes, and design clarifications.

---

## Data Steward

### Role Summary
The Data Steward owns data definitions, quality, and lifecycle practices to ensure reliable data across products and analytics.

### Responsibilities
- Define and maintain canonical data definitions and schemas.
- Establish data quality checks and monitoring.
- Define data retention, access, and privacy practices in collaboration with Security/Compliance.

### Goals
- Improve data reliability and trust for decision-making.
- Reduce data-related rework and defects.

### Typical Communication
- Data governance meetings, schema change notifications, and analytics handoffs.

---

## Security / Compliance Owner

### Role Summary
The Security / Compliance Owner ensures features meet security and regulatory requirements, drives threat modeling, and coordinates audit readiness.

### Responsibilities
- Review feature designs and release checklists for security and compliance.
- Coordinate threat modeling and security assessments.
- Own remediation prioritization and verification.

### Goals
- Reduce security incidents and audit findings.
- Ensure compliance obligations are met before release.

### Typical Communication
- Security reviews, vulnerability reports, and compliance checkpoints.

---

## Onboarding Coordinator

### Role Summary
The Onboarding Coordinator ensures new team members and stakeholders understand processes, access, and documentation, and maintains onboarding materials.

### Responsibilities
- Maintain and update process documentation and onboarding checklists.
- Schedule and run orientation sessions for new contributors.
- Track completion of required training and access provisioning.

### Goals
- Reduce ramp time for new contributors.
- Ensure consistent understanding of processes.

### Typical Communication
- Onboarding sessions, documentation updates, and HR coordination.
